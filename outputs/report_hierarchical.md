## 📣 SECTION 8 — INSTRUCTIONS TO THE COMMAND POST

The following directives are issued by the Incident Commander to all Command Post personnel and on-scene supervisors, effective immediately:

---

### 🔴 IMMEDIATE PRIORITY ACTIONS

1. **CONFIRM ROUTE CLEARANCE STATUS**
Contact Police Traffic patrol lead on **Channel 3** immediately. Obtain confirmation that Main Street. and Pine Street. approach corridors are open or receive an updated ETA for clearance. Relay status to all inbound Fire and Ambulance Service units without delay.

2. **ESTABLISH UNIFIED ON-SCENE COMMAND**
All department supervisors — Fire, Medical, Police — are directed to check in with the **Police Tactical Van** upon arrival at 45 Pine Street (WC1A Bloomsbury, London) A unified on-scene command structure must be operational before any interior rescue operations are authorized on floors above ground level.

3. **OBTAIN SCENE TRIAGE REPORT — PRIORITY REQUEST**
The first Rapid Response Vehicle (RRV) on scene is directed to transmit a **preliminary casualty count and triage category breakdown** to Command Post as soon as it is safely possible. This data is critical for:
   - Determining whether burn unit mutual aid must be activated
   - Deciding whether medical and fire reserves should be released
   - Updating hospital receiving teams with accurate patient volume

4. **hazardous materials CLEARANCE GATE — DO NOT BYPASS**
Interior rescue operations above the ground floor are **suspended** pending hazardous materials unit air quality assessment results. Fire Rescue Units are authorized for ground-floor operations only until hazardous materials clears upper floors or confirms respiratory protection requirements. This is a non-negotiable safety gate.

---

### 🟡 SECONDARY PRIORITY ACTIONS

5. **MONITOR ST. JUDE BURN UNIT CAPACITY**
Medical Dispatch is actively tracking St. Jude Medical's 4-bed burn unit. Command Post must be prepared to issue a **regional mutual aid burn center request** on short notice. Pre-identify the nearest regional burn facility now — do not wait for capacity to be exceeded before initiating contact.

6. **MANAGE CIVILIAN EVACUATION FLOW**
Police patrol cars are holding the perimeter at 45 Pine Street (WC1A Bloomsbury, London) Command Post should coordinate with Police to designate a **civilian assembly point** at a safe distance from the structure, away from emergency vehicle access routes. Ensure Medical RRVs have clear access to any civilians presenting with smoke inhalation symptoms at the perimeter.

7. **COMMUNICATIONS DISCIPLINE — CHANNEL ASSIGNMENTS**
   - **Channel 1:** Incident Command — All Department Supervisors
   - **Channel 2:** Fire Operations — Internal Fire/Rescue Coordination
   - **Channel 3:** Traffic & Routing — Police Traffic patrols / All Inbound Units
   - **Channel 4:** Medical Operations — Ambulance Service / Hospital Coordination
   - **Channel 5:** hazardous materials — Dedicated hazardous materials Assessment Channel

8. **RESERVE ACTIVATION AUTHORITY**
Reserve units across all departments **may not be self-activated** by individual department supervisors. All reserve activation requests must be routed through **Command Post — Channel 1** for Incident Commander authorization. This ensures coordinated escalation and prevents resource fragmentation.
