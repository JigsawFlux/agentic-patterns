## 📣 SECTION 8 — INSTRUCTIONS TO THE COMMAND POST

The following directives are issued by the Incident Commander to all Command Post personnel and on-scene supervisors, effective immediately:

---

### 🔴 IMMEDIATE PRIORITY ACTIONS

1. **CONFIRM ROUTE CLEARANCE STATUS**
Contact Police Traffic Unit lead on **Channel 3** immediately. Obtain confirmation that Main St. and Pine St. approach corridors are open or receive an updated ETA for clearance. Relay status to all inbound Fire and EMS units without delay.

2. **ESTABLISH UNIFIED ON-SCENE COMMAND**
All department supervisors — Fire, Medical, Police — are directed to check in with the **Police Tactical Van** upon arrival at 45 Pine St (WC1A Bloomsbury, London) A unified on-scene command structure must be operational before any interior rescue operations are authorized on floors above ground level.

3. **OBTAIN SCENE TRIAGE REPORT — PRIORITY REQUEST**
The first Paramedic Fly-Car on scene is directed to transmit a **preliminary casualty count and triage category breakdown** to Command Post as soon as it is safely possible. This data is critical for:
   - Determining whether burn unit mutual aid must be activated
   - Deciding whether medical and fire reserves should be released
   - Updating hospital receiving teams with accurate patient volume

4. **HAZMAT CLEARANCE GATE — DO NOT BYPASS**
Interior rescue operations above the ground floor are **suspended** pending Hazmat unit air quality assessment results. Fire Rescue Squads are authorized for ground-floor operations only until Hazmat clears upper floors or confirms respiratory protection requirements. This is a non-negotiable safety gate.

---

### 🟡 SECONDARY PRIORITY ACTIONS

5. **MONITOR ST. JUDE BURN UNIT CAPACITY**
Medical Dispatch is actively tracking St. Jude Medical's 4-bed burn unit. Command Post must be prepared to issue a **regional mutual aid burn center request** on short notice. Pre-identify the nearest regional burn facility now — do not wait for capacity to be exceeded before initiating contact.

6. **MANAGE CIVILIAN EVACUATION FLOW**
Police Cruisers are holding the perimeter at 45 Pine St (WC1A Bloomsbury, London) Command Post should coordinate with Police to designate a **civilian assembly point** at a safe distance from the structure, away from emergency vehicle access routes. Ensure Medical Fly-Cars have clear access to any civilians presenting with smoke inhalation symptoms at the perimeter.

7. **COMMUNICATIONS DISCIPLINE — CHANNEL ASSIGNMENTS**
   - **Channel 1:** Incident Command — All Department Supervisors
   - **Channel 2:** Fire Operations — Internal Fire/Rescue Coordination
   - **Channel 3:** Traffic & Routing — Police Traffic Units / All Inbound Units
   - **Channel 4:** Medical Operations — EMS / Hospital Coordination
   - **Channel 5:** Hazmat — Dedicated Hazmat Assessment Channel

8. **RESERVE ACTIVATION AUTHORITY**
Reserve units across all departments **may not be self-activated** by individual department supervisors. All reserve activation requests must be routed through **Command Post — Channel 1** for Incident Commander authorization. This ensures coordinated escalation and prevents resource fragmentation.

---

### 🟢 ONGOING MONITORING DIRECTIVES

9. **ROLLING STATUS UPDATES:** All department supervisors are to provide Command Post with a status update every **10 minutes** until the scene is declared controlled.

10. **DOCUMENTATION:** All dispatch decisions, resource movements, hospital routing, and communications are to be logged in real time for post-incident review and after-action reporting.
